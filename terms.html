4. AI-generated content & safety guardrails

Imagine: Kids Coloring Book uses OpenAI's image-generation API to create coloring pages from the text prompts typed or spoken in the app. To keep the experience appropriate for children, we apply multiple layers of safety:

  • On-device prompt filter. Before any prompt is sent off the device, it is screened against a content-safety filter that blocks sexual content, violence, weapons, drugs, hate speech, self-harm, prompt-injection attempts, and gibberish. Blocked prompts never leave the device.
  • Style enforcement. Every prompt is automatically wrapped with instructions that constrain the output to a black-and-white, kid-appropriate coloring-book illustration. A child cannot override this style through wording.
  • OpenAI moderation. OpenAI applies its own safety classifiers on top of ours. Requests it considers unsafe are refused by the service and the child is shown a gentle "try something else" message.
  • Parental gate. Purchases, external links, and sharing a drawing to another app are all protected by an arithmetic challenge designed to be solvable by adults but not by young children.
  • No social surface. The app has no chat, no comments, no public gallery, and no user accounts, so children cannot contact strangers through the app and strangers cannot contact them.

AI-generated output can occasionally still be imperfect, unexpected, or fail to match the prompt. We cannot guarantee that every generated image will be suitable; a parent should be available to supervise. If you ever encounter inappropriate output, please report it to garrettmichael07@icloud.com and we will investigate and tighten our filters.
